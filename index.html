The RTW Crisis

Why U.S. Navy shipbuilding is stuck

The Navy needs a 32% fleet increase, but shipyards can't see beyond an 8-week planning horizon. The breakdown isn't workforce — it's Release-to-Work.

8 weeks

Planning blind spot

Reliable build plans at major U.S. shipyards don't extend beyond 8-10 weeks. Beyond that horizon, work is planned by workstation loading rather than named work packages — a manual, error-prone process.

~5,000

Vendor exodus

Only 5,000 small and mid-sized manufacturers serve the Navy out of 60,000-70,000 capable firms nationwide. Vendors leave because today's system forces their best people to decipher processes instead of making parts.

100x

Unpredictable supply chain

Parts arrive late, incomplete, or defective — installed just to keep the schedule, then ripped out and reworked at far greater cost. Commercial component costs can be 100x higher inside the MIB.
